Which, traitor, thou wouldst have me answer to. Let Aesop fable in a winter's night; His currish riddle sorts not with this place. Nay, take away this scolding crookback rather. I know my duty; you are all undutiful. Lascivious Edward, and thou perjur'd George, And thou misshapen Dick, I tell ye all I am your better, traitors as ye are; And thou usurp'st my father's right and mine.
